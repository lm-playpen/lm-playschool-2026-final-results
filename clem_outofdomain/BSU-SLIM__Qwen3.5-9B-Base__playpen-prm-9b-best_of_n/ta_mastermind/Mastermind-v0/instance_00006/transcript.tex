\texttt{        *   `Guess{[}4{]}=2`: Since `Code{[}4{]}≠2` (from Turn 1 constraint), this is a **White** peg (2 is in code, but not at Pos 4).} \\
\texttt{    *   This accounts for **2 Whites**.} \\
\texttt{    *   Therefore, the **1 Black** peg must come from the remaining position:} \\
\texttt{        *   `Guess{[}1{]}=5`: Must be `Code{[}1{]}=5` (Black).} \\
\texttt{        *   OR `Guess{[}2{]}=6`: Must be `Code{[}2{]}=6` (Black).} \\
\texttt{    *   Since we have 1 Black and 2 Whites, one of these must be true, and the other must *not* be true (otherwise we'd have 2 Blacks).} \\
\texttt{    *   If `Code{[}1{]}=5`:} \\
\texttt{        *   Then `Guess{[}2{]}=6` must be a White (so `Code{[}2{]}≠6`).} \\
\texttt{        *   But we know from Turn 1 that `Code{[}2{]}≠2`, `Code{[}3{]}≠3`, `Code{[}4{]}≠4`. Does `Code{[}2{]}≠6` hold?} \\
\texttt{        *   If `Code{[}1{]}=5`, then 5 is used. The code needs two digits from \{5,6\}. So the other digit must be 6.} \\
\texttt{        *   So `Code` would be `5 6 ? ?`.} \\
\texttt{        *   We have 5 at Pos 1 (Black).} \\
\texttt{        *   We have 6 at Pos 2. If `Code{[}2{]}=6`, that would be a **Black** peg too.} \\
\texttt{        *   That would give us 1 Black (from Pos 1) + 1 Black (from Pos 2) + 2 Whites = 3 Black, 2 White.} \\
\texttt{        *   But we only have **1 Black, 2 White**.} \\
\texttt{        *   Therefore, `Code{[}2{]}` cannot be 6.} \\
\texttt{        *   So if `Code{[}1{]}=5`, then `Code{[}2{]}≠6`.} \\
\texttt{    *} \\
