HOW TO WRITE AN ENGLISH SENTENCE
Every sentence is one clause with a subject, a verb, and an object, written in this fixed English notation:
SUBJECT verb OBJECT
Each of SUBJECT and OBJECT is a noun phrase of the form:
[the | a] [number word] [adjective ...] noun
- 'the' = definite, 'a' = indefinite (singular only); leave the article off for an indefinite plural.
- a number word (two, three) sets the count; with no number word the noun is singular.
Examples of well-formed English sentences you may send:
a dog carry a bird
three dogs carry a bird

HOW THE SESSION WORKS
1. INVESTIGATION: You have 4 rounds of questioning. In each round you may ask
the informant to translate up to 3 English sentences (so at most
12 probes in total). The informant answers each one truthfully and
exactly. Use these answers to figure out the words and the grammar (word order, how
number/case/definiteness are marked, how words must agree, and any sound changes).
Choose your probes deliberately - test specific hypotheses.
2. EXAM: After the investigation rounds, the informant will give you 10 NEW

space must be right (capitalisation and surrounding spaces are ignored). The exam
sentences will use the same vocabulary but in combinations you have not been shown, so
you must induce the general rules, not just memorise examples.
