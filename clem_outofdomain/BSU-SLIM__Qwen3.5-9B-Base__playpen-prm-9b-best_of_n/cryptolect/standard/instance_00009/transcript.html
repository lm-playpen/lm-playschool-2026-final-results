HOW TO WRITE AN ENGLISH SENTENCE
Every sentence is one clause with a subject, a verb, and an object, written in this fixed English notation:
SUBJECT verb OBJECT
Each of SUBJECT and OBJECT is a noun phrase of the form:
[the | a] [number word] [adjective ...] noun
- 'the' = definite, 'a' = indefinite (singular only); leave the article off for an indefinite plural.
- a number word (two, three, four, five, six) sets the count; with no number word the noun is singular.
- zero or more adjectives may come before the noun.
Examples of well-formed English sentences you may send:
