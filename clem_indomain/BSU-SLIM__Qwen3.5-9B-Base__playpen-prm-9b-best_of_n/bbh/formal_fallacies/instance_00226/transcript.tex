\texttt{*   \$S(x)\$: \$x\$ is a schoolmate of Karen.} \\
\texttt{*   \$I(x)\$: \$x\$ is a sister of Ruth.} \\
\\ 
\texttt{**Premise 1:** "Nobody is neither a classmate of Marisa nor a schoolmate of Karen."} \\
\texttt{The phrase "neither A nor B" means someone lacks both A and B. The statement says *nobody* has this property.} \\
\texttt{Logically, this is equivalent to saying: It is not the case that (\$x\$ is not a classmate AND \$x\$ is not a schoolmate).} \\
\texttt{Formal translation: \$\textbackslash{}neg \textbackslash{}forall x (\textbackslash{}neg C(x) \textbackslash{}land \textbackslash{}neg S(x))\$} \\
\texttt{Equivalently, by De Morgan's laws and quantifier negation: \$\textbackslash{}forall x \textbackslash{}neg (\textbackslash{}neg C(x) \textbackslash{}land \textbackslash{}neg S(x))\$, which simplifies to \$\textbackslash{}forall x (C(x) \textbackslash{}lor S(x))\$.} \\
\texttt{*Meaning:* Everyone is either a classmate of Marisa or a schoolmate of Karen (or both).} \\
\\ 
\texttt{**Premise 2:** "Not every sister of Ruth is a schoolmate of Karen."} \\
\texttt{This means there exists at least one sister of Ruth who is *not* a schoolmate of Karen.} \\
\texttt{Formal translation: \$\textbackslash{}exists x (I(x) \textbackslash{}land \textbackslash{}neg S(x))\$.} \\
\texttt{*Meaning:* There is at least one person who is a sister of Ruth and is not a schoolmate of Karen.} \\
\\ 
\texttt{**Conclusion:** "Somebody is a sister of Ruth and not a classmate of Marisa."} \\
\texttt{Formal translation: \$\textbackslash{}exists x (I(x) \textbackslash{}land \textbackslash{}neg C(x))\$.} \\
\\ 
\texttt{**2. Analyze the Deductive Logic:**} \\
\\ 
\texttt{*   From **Premise 1**, we know that for every person \$x\$, they must satisfy \$C(x) \textbackslash{}lor S(x)\$.} \\
\texttt{*   From **Premise 2**, we know there exists a specific person, let's call her \$a\$, such that \$I(a)\$ is true and \$S(a)\$ is false (\$\textbackslash{}neg S(a)\$).} \\
\texttt{*   Now, apply Premise 1 to this specific person \$a\$:} \\
\texttt{    *   Since \$S(a)\$ is false, for the disjunction \$C(a) \textbackslash{}lor S(a)\$ to be true (which it must be, per Premise 1), the other part of the disjunction, \$C(a)\$, **must** be true.} \\
\texttt{    *   Therefore, \$C(a)\$ is true.} \\
\texttt{*   Since we already know \$I(a)\$ is true (from Premise 2) and we have just deduced that \$C(a)\$ is true, it follows that \$\textbackslash{}neg C(a)\$ is **false**.} \\
\texttt{*   The conclusion claims that there exists someone who is a sister (\$I(x)\$) AND not a classmate (\$\textbackslash{}neg C(x)\$).} \\

\\
